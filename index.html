Selected Work

Digital Marketing / SMB Operations

Social Media Content Automation

Challenge

A growing business needed to maintain consistent social media presence across 5+ platforms without dedicated marketing staff. Content creation was ad-hoc, posting was manual, and there was no connection between content performance and business outcomes.

Approach

Designed an AI-powered content workflow: automated content generation based on business context, intelligent scheduling with optimal timing, multi-platform publishing from single source, approval gates for quality control.

Outcome

Reduced content creation time by 70%. Established repeatable publishing cadence. Created clear ownership: business owner approves, system executes.

E-commerce / Fashion Retail

Virtual Try-On System

Challenge

An online fashion retailer faced high return rates (>30%) because customers couldn't visualize how clothing would look on them. Previous "virtual fitting" solutions were gimmicks that didn't affect purchase behavior.

Approach

Built a practical virtual try-on system: realistic outfit visualization on user's body type, integration with existing product catalog, seamless checkout experience, explicit measurement tracking correlation between widget usage and return rates.

Outcome

Widget deployed to production store. System designed with clear metrics: widget engagement → conversion rate → return rate impact. Ownership assigned to merchandising team.

Consumer Tech / Personal Productivity

Digital Legacy System

Challenge

An individual wanted to preserve and structure their digital knowledge — documents, notes, decisions, personal history — in a way that could be useful for themselves and potentially their family.

Approach

Designed a personal AI system: ingests and organizes disparate digital content, creates searchable AI-queryable knowledge base, maintains privacy with local-first architecture, simple enough to use daily.

Outcome

Working prototype with local-first architecture. Clear scope: what stays private, what can be shared. User owns their data and the system that processes it.

Logistics / B2B Services

Conversational AI for Customer Support

Challenge

A logistics company received hundreds of daily customer inquiries about shipment status, pricing, and delivery windows. Support staff spent 80% of time on repetitive queries, leaving complex issues unaddressed.

Approach

Designed a conversational AI layer: connected to real-time shipment tracking, handled standard queries without human intervention, escalated complex cases with full context, tracked deflection rate and satisfaction.

Outcome

Reduced routine query load on support team. Established clear handoff protocol: AI handles what it can, humans handle what they should. Metrics defined: response time, deflection rate, escalation quality.

Developer Tools / Productivity

macOS Productivity Utilities

Challenge

Presenters and educators needed better cursor visibility during screen sharing and recordings. Existing solutions were either too complex or didn't work reliably on macOS.

Approach

Built focused, single-purpose utilities: cursor spotlight for visibility during presentations, minimal system resource usage, native macOS integration. Philosophy: do one thing well, ship it, move on.

Outcome

Published to App Store. Demonstrates approach: not every problem needs AI. Sometimes the right answer is a simple, well-designed tool.

Content Production / Creative Automation

AI-Powered Creative Production

Watch demo: 53 lines → .mp4

Challenge

A content team needed to produce short animated videos quickly without specialized animation skills or expensive production pipelines. Traditional workflows required multiple specialists and weeks of coordination.

Approach

Built an end-to-end automated pipeline: 53 lines of prompt → finished .mp4 lands on your desktop. AI-generated poetry/script, visual generation through specialized image models, automated video assembly. Multiple AI models orchestrated deterministically under Fractalic AI orchestration.

Manufacturing / 3D Printing Services

Autonomous Sales Pipeline

Challenge

A 3D printing company handled hundreds of small orders (<$1,000) manually. Each deal required: lead qualification, quote generation, task assignment to operators, customer follow-ups, and payment tracking. Staff spent 80% of time on process, not production.

Approach

Designed and deployed a HubSpot automation system that runs the entire pipeline autonomously: receives inbound leads, qualifies and routes automatically, assigns production tasks to operators, sends status updates at each stage, tracks to payment. Human intervention only for exceptions.

Outcome

End-to-end automation from first contact to payment received. Operations team refocused on production quality instead of admin. Clear metrics: deals processed, time-to-close, human intervention rate. Bot manages flow, humans manage exceptions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "practical AI" mean?

AI that connects to real business outcomes — revenue, cost, risk, time. Not demos, not experiments without owners, not technology for its own sake.

How is Fractalic Studio different from AI consultancies?

We don't sell transformation narratives. We map your existing structure, find where AI fits, and design systems with clear ownership. We build ourselves out, not in.

What size companies do you work with?

Primarily 5-150 people. Large enough to have real processes and constraints. Small enough that founders and leadership still own key decisions.

Do you implement AI tools or just advise?

Both. We design the system (processes, roles, metrics) and can support implementation. But we always leave you with documentation and governance to run it without us.

How long does a typical engagement last?

AI Opportunity Audit: 2 weeks. Operating Blueprint: 4-6 weeks. Implementation Advisory: ongoing retainer with defined scope.

What industries do you specialize in?

We focus on business structure, not industry. Our work spans e-commerce, logistics, professional services, and SaaS. The common thread: founders who want AI under control, not AI controlling them.

Do you work with startups?

Yes, if you have real revenue and repeatable work. If you're pre-product or still searching for product-market fit, AI strategy is premature.

What happens after the engagement ends?

You own everything: the documentation, the decision logs, the governance framework. We design for exit, not dependency.

About Fractalic Studio

Fractalic Studio was created for founders who think in systems and are tired of vague AI promises.

Our background is in strategy, operating models, and power structures inside organisations: who actually decides, who owns which outcomes, and how processes hold under stress.

We bring that lens to AI. Tools matter, but only inside a clear frame of authority, responsibility, and measurement.

Principles

No hype

We do not promise transformation, disruption, or "reinventing your business". We commit to specific changes in how work is done and measured.

System over tools

Tools are interchangeable. The system — processes, roles, decision rights — is what matters. We design for replaceable tools inside a stable frame.

Authority first

Every AI workflow has an owner. If there is no clear owner on the client side, we treat the initiative as a risk by default.

Explicit trade-offs

Every recommendation comes with what it will cost: time, attention, political capital, and maintenance.

Measured or removed

If an AI initiative cannot be meaningfully measured in terms the business cares about, it should not outlive an experiment.

Exit built in

We do not design dependence on ourselves. We build documentation, decision logs, and simple governance so the client can run the system without us.
